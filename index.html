History of Tennis

Origins

Tennis originated in France during the 12th century with a game called "jeu de paume." This evolved into the sport known today, with racquets introduced in the 16th century. The modern game of lawn tennis started in England in the late 19th century.

(Source: History of Tennis)

Modern Era

Development of the Open Era

The Open Era of tennis began in 1968 when tournaments allowed both amateurs and professionals to compete, leading to international fame for champions like Rod Laver and Billie Jean King.

Technological Advances

20th and 21st-century advancements, such as synthetic strings and graphite racquets, have transformed tennis, increasing both speed and power.

Grand Slam Tournaments

The Four Majors

The four Grand Slam tournaments—Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon, and the US Open—are the pinnacle of tennis, drawing top players worldwide.

Wimbledon

Wimbledon, first held in 1877, is known for its traditions, including the all-white dress code and grass courts, making it an iconic sports event.

The Evolution of the Game

Tennis continues to evolve with innovations like Hawk-Eye technology, making it one of the world’s most popular sports.
